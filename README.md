# Environmental and Housing Factors Associated with Adult Asthma Outcomes Across Bronx Neighborhoods

## Project Background 
Adult asthma remains a significant public health concern in urban communities, where environmental exposures and housing conditions may contribute to poor respiratory outcomes. This project analyzes neighborhood-level adult asthma hospitalization rates across Bronx neighborhoods to understand whether air pollution patterns and housing maintenance conditions are associated with higher asthma burden. The goal is to identify localized disparities that may inform targeted public health interventions rather than borough-wide approaches.

### Executive Summary

#### Overview of Findings
This analysis finds that adult asthma hospitalization rates are unevenly distributed across Bronx neighborhoods, with a small subset experiencing significantly higher burden. Neighborhoods with elevated air pollution levels and poorer housing maintenance conditions tend to show higher asthma hospitalization rates. These patterns suggest that asthma risk is shaped by localized environmental and housing factors rather than borough-wide trends. The findings support the need for neighborhood-targeted public health and housing interventions.
